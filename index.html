Capture

Get everything out of your head and into a trusted external system. 100% capture is the goal — your brain is for having ideas, not holding them.

The Core Rule

If it's on your mind, it's not getting done. Capture it externally so your mind is free to think, not to remember.

Physical Inbox

Paper tray on your desk. Toss in mail, notes, receipts, printouts — anything physical that needs processing.

Digital Inbox

A note-taking app, email inbox, or task app. Quick-capture thoughts, ideas, and commitments as they come.

Voice / On-the-go

Voice memos, pocket notebook, phone camera. Capture in the moment so nothing slips through the cracks.

Triggers Checklist

Walk through areas of life & work to surface open loops: commitments, projects, waiting-fors, someday ideas.

Key Habits

Use as few inboxes as possible, but as many as needed. Empty them regularly (in the Clarify phase). Never use your inbox as a to-do list.

Clarify / Process

Take each item from your inbox and decide: What is it? Is it actionable? Transform vague stuff into clear next actions, projects, or reference.

The Core Rule

Process top-down, one item at a time. Never put anything back into your inbox — decide and move it.

Organize

Place clarified items into the right buckets. Each outcome of the Clarify phase has a home. Your system only works if everything is in its place.

The Core Rule

Organize by action required, not by topic or priority. The categories below are all you need.

Projects List

  • Any outcome requiring 2+ actions
  • Define the desired outcome clearly
  • Every project must have a next action
  • Review weekly to keep current

Next Actions

  • The very next physical, visible step
  • Organized by context
  • @Computer, @Phone, @Office
  • @Errands, @Home, @Anywhere

Waiting For

  • Delegated actions you're tracking
  • Record what, to whom, and when
  • Follow up during weekly review
  • Move to Next Actions when returned

Calendar

  • Date-specific actions only (hard landscape)
  • Day-specific information
  • Time-specific appointments
  • Do NOT use as a wish list

Someday / Maybe

  • Ideas you're not committed to yet
  • Books, trips, skills, projects to consider
  • "Parking lot" for creative ideas
  • Review regularly — activate or drop

Reference

  • Non-actionable but useful information
  • Files, articles, manuals, contacts
  • Must be easily retrievable
  • General or topic-specific filing
Project Support Material

Plans, notes, research, and other material for active projects. Keep separate from actions and reference. Organize per-project, not per-topic.

Reflect / Review

Regularly step back to keep your system current, complete, and trustworthy. The Weekly Review is the critical success factor of GTD.

Daily Review

Check your calendar for hard commitments. Scan your Next Actions lists by context. Adapt as the day evolves.

The Weekly Review (~1-2 hours)
1

Get Clear

Collect loose papers & materials. Empty all inboxes (email, notes, voicemail). Process every item to zero.

2

Get Current

Review Next Actions — mark off completed, add new ones. Review calendar (past 2 weeks for loose ends, upcoming 2 weeks for prep). Review Waiting For — follow up as needed. Review Projects list — ensure each has a next action. Review checklists for any triggered actions.

3

Get Creative

Review Someday/Maybe list — activate, keep, or remove items. Think about new ideas, goals, or projects. Be creative and courageous — evaluate from a higher perspective.

Engage / Do

With a trusted system, you can choose what to do with confidence. Use these four criteria to decide what to work on right now.

The Core Rule

Trust your system. If you've captured, clarified, organized, and reviewed, you can be present with whatever you choose to do — guilt-free.

4 Criteria for Choosing Actions
1

Context

What can you do here with the tools available? Filter your Next Actions by @context.

2

Time

How much time do you have before your next commitment? Pick actions that fit the window.

3

Energy

What is your current energy level? Match the action to your mental and physical state.

4

Priority

Given context, time, and energy — what will give the highest return? Trust your intuition.

3 Models for Daily Work

Pre-defined Work

Working through your Next Actions lists and calendar. The planned, known tasks you've already captured and organized.

Work as It Appears

Handling unexpected inputs — a colleague's request, urgent email, a sudden problem. Conscious choice to engage or capture for later.

Defining Your Work

Processing your inboxes, clarifying items, planning projects. The "meta-work" that keeps your system running.

The Payoff

A "mind like water" — a state of readiness with no residual tension. You respond appropriately to whatever shows up because you trust that nothing is falling through the cracks.
